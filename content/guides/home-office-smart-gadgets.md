Working from home has become normal, but a productive home office is more than a desk and a laptop. The right gadgets can reduce small annoyances, improve comfort, and make deep work easier to protect.

## Start with ergonomics

Your chair, desk height, keyboard, mouse, and monitor position shape how your body feels after a full day of work. Before buying flashy devices, fix the setup you touch for hours every day.

- Adjustable chair with lumbar support
- Monitor at eye level
- Keyboard and mouse that keep wrists neutral
- Standing option for posture variation

## Use technology to reduce interruptions

Noise-canceling headphones, reliable lighting, a good webcam, and a simple docking setup reduce the number of small problems you solve before every meeting.

The best office gadgets disappear into the workflow. They make your setup more consistent without adding complexity.

## Buy for your actual work style

A creator may need lighting and audio first. A spreadsheet-heavy operator may benefit more from an ultrawide monitor, precision mouse, and keyboard. Choose products around the work you repeat most often.
