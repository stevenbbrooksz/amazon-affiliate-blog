Energy bills are a recurring cost, and small habits can add up. Smart home devices help by automating schedules, reducing standby power, and giving you better visibility into usage.

## Start with the biggest energy categories

Heating, cooling, lighting, and always-on electronics usually matter most. A smart thermostat or plug will not fix every bill, but it can help remove obvious waste.

## Automation beats intention

Most people intend to turn devices off. Automation makes that behavior consistent. Timers, occupancy sensors, and schedules are useful because they work even when you forget.

- Smart thermostat schedules
- Smart plugs for standby devices
- LED bulbs with timers
- Motion sensors for low-traffic rooms

## Check compatibility before buying

Some devices need a hub, neutral wire, specific HVAC wiring, or a particular app ecosystem. Read compatibility details before clicking buy, especially for thermostats and wall switches.
